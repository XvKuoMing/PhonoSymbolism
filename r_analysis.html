Preambula

Numerous studies has been dedicated to the area of brand-naming. Some of the researches are trying to discover the correlation between brand names likelihood and the sounds they use. Such attempt to map all concepts to specific sounds, is called sound mapping. In the following research:

Trninić, Z. (2023). Creating effective brand names with sound symbolic mappings. Strategic Management-International Journal of Strategic Management and Decision Support Systems in Strategic Management, 28(2).

author has been investigated that different phonemes are associated with different qualities of objects. For example, active articulators such as /p/, /b/, /m/ are uttered more frequently when it comes to large objects. Despite the fact that results of the paper’s investigation indeed justify the hypothesis, the methodology is quit debatable. The most fallible part if presumption is that chosen products i.e. razors and soaps are associated with nothing more but sharpness ans softness. The problem is that razor are actually might be associated with softness, since for some people it is desirable to not get a scratch from using it. The aim of this paper is to conduct similar experiment but with notion that the way people associate products is relative to each respondent. Hence, the design of new experiment is the following: 1. For each respondent we generate 6 unique combinations of words. The very words’ structure is CV-CV-CV, where each CV is from common categories. 2. Then we ask to give an association for chosen word 3. and at last we ask to chose the word again

Data Description

Main data: user_id - simply an id of respondent firstly_chosen_group - the category of a word that was chosen by a user from the start of survey associations - chosen word’s associations that user came up with image - a descriptive image of chosen word that user came up with is_interested - yes/no answer to the question: would you be interested in a product simply because of the name you chose lastly_chosen_group - the category of a word that was chosen by a user at the end of survey

features data: transformed main data where associations and image column’s values were used to calculate their own phonetic group stats

Tests

main test

Since we are dealing with categorical variables and the size of samples is relativelty small, then it is a good idea to use Fisher Test:
H0: the last choice of word group is NOT influenced by choice of first word group
H1: the last choice of word group is influenced by choice of first word group
fisher.test(data.tabs)
## 
##  Fisher's Exact Test for Count Data
## 
## data:  data.tabs
## p-value = 0.1299
## alternative hypothesis: two.sided
The p-value is > 0.05, which gives us a reason to accept h0. However, we should also check consonants and vowels independently
